Age and Gender prediction

  • ➦ Dataset Selection and Preprocessing: The project utilized the UTK Face dataset, a diverse collection of facial images annotated with age, gender, and ethnicity information. The dataset was preprocessed to extract relevant features and normalize images for training the Convolutional Neural Network (CNN). Preprocessing steps involved resizing, cropping, and augmenting the images to enhance the model's ability to generalize.

  • ➦ Architecture and Training of CNN: A Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) architecture was designed to capture intricate features in facial images for age and gender prediction. The model consisted of multiple convolutional layers followed by max-pooling, batch normalization, and fully connected layers. The network was trained using a subset of the UTK Face dataset, and hyperparameters were fine-tuned to achieve an impressive accuracy of 89%.

  • ➦ Age and Gender Prediction Performance: The trained CNN demonstrated a remarkable accuracy of 89% in predicting both age and gender from facial images. The model's robustness was validated through rigorous testing on a separate test set, ensuring its ability to generalize to unseen data. The accuracy metric was chosen as it provides a comprehensive evaluation of the model's performance in identifying both age and gender categories.

  • ➦ Challenges and Future Improvements: The project also addressed challenges encountered during the development process, such as data imbalance, overfitting, and computational constraints. Strategies like data augmentation, dropout layers, and model optimization were employed to mitigate these challenges. In terms of future improvements, considerations included expanding the dataset, fine-tuning the model further, and exploring more advanced architectures to achieve even higher prediction accuracy.
